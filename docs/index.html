Antique Fintech Conference 2026: Monetisation & Merchant Lending ThemesNEW

02 Jul 2026 · Broker Research · Sector Report · Antique Fintech Conference 2026 - 260702.pdf
📌 Crux of the Report
  • Antique hosted 18 fintechs and industry experts (2 Jul 2026) across payments, banking infrastructure, RegTech and digital lending; core message: the next growth phase will be driven by monetisation, integrated platforms and disciplined execution rather than payment-volume or account growth alone
  • Merchant loan asset quality has improved considerably over 2 years: Paytm flags declining expected credit loss, Mobikwik cites lower slippages/better recoveries for 2 quarters, FLDG guarantees now capped at 5% with Mobikwik's outstanding FLDG losses down ~35% YoY; consumer fintech lending stress post-2023 is described as stabilized
  • Payments economics improving: merchant payment margins expanded from ~3bps to ~4bps heading to ~5bps (Paytm guides 20-25% FY27 revenue growth, mid-50s contribution margin; 35% merchant GMV growth vs ~30% industry); Mobikwik claims ~20% wallet share and 14-16bps payment monetisation; MDR on UPI visibility remains limited
  • NBFC deep-dives: Creditwise Capital (2W lender) - AUM INR 7bn, 20-21% IRR, credit cost 2.5-2.6% on conservative 360-DPD write-off, CRAR 27%, CRISIL BBB with BBB+ upgrade expected, HDFC Bank/Muthoot Capital co-lending (80:20, no FLDG), equity raise to lift net worth to INR 4.5bn; Moneyboxx - pivot from 94% unsecured (FY23) to 67% secured disbursements, GNPA down to 3.59% from 6.61% (ARC-aided), NIM compressed to 13.9%, PAT just INR 13mn, targeting INR 12.5bn AUM by FY27
  • Infrastructure/RegTech demand strong: M2P (200+ banks, ~50% international revenue) near break-even, targeting 25-30% EBITDA margin in 2-3 years and possible listing in 3-4 years; IDfy at ~INR 2.5bn revenue/~5% EBITDA margin targets INR 8-10bn at 30-40% margin over 3-4 years; DiGiSPICE/Spice Money leads Off-Us AEPS (18.4% share), FY26 PAT INR 254mn with credit disbursements up 2.8x
  • New-age brokers (Dhan, Upstox, Univest) describe a maturing cycle: demat accounts ~26 crore (up ~6x in 8 years) but SEBI F&O curbs pushed diversification into MTF, mutual funds, wealth and lending; Upstox reports ~INR 1,200cr revenue (~50% F&O), fourth straight profitable year; Dhan (unicorn at $1.2bn, Oct 2025) remains 78% derivatives-revenue-heavy but says no fund raise needed for 2 years
  • Other pipeline names: Pine Labs (~70% POS share, ~85% in-store affordability share, 24-26% FY27 revenue growth guidance, INR 27bn cash), Zaggle (25-30% standalone / ~40% consolidated FY27 growth, cash-flow break-even targeted this year, investor caveat on Propel gross-revenue accounting), Revolut India (PPI + AD2 licences, 12k users onboarded, 1 lakh target in 3-4 months), GetePay (300 bank integrations, profitable since inception), Upsure (AI insurance-underwriting platform)
  • PhonePe (13mn+ merchants, Unity SFB JV, new PA licence) operates as an LSP without its own NBFC - capital-light growth; merchant loans priced ~28% initially with 6-8% first loss; fintechs dominate the INR 50k-3 lakh ticket segment where banks stay cautious
⚠ Risk Analysis 3
● WATCH
Moneyboxx's pivot from 94% unsecured (FY23) to 67% secured disbursements is de-risking the book, but profitability is wafer-thin: FY26 PAT of just INR 13mn on INR 2,321mn income, NIM compressed to 13.9% (from 16.3%) as lending IRR fell to 26.0%, and GNPA improvement to 3.59% was partly ARC-transaction-aided
A BBB/Stable (CRISIL and India Ratings) micro-enterprise lender mid-pivot: the secured book's ~2-year vintage is unseasoned and headline GNPA is flattered by the ARC sale. Livestock is 53.1% of AUM - a differentiated but monoline-adjacent exposure. Track collection efficiency (95.7% secured, 93.5% overall in Q4FY26), credit cost vs the ~3.5% stated peak, and progress toward the INR 12.5bn FY27 AUM target without diluting yield further
● WATCH
SEBI's 2025 F&O tightening cut broking industry volumes ~13% overnight and has forced structural diversification - brokers with 50-78% derivatives revenue concentration (Upstox ~50%, Dhan ~78%) are pushing into MTF, mutual funds, lending and wealth to defend earnings
Regulatory revenue concentration risk remains the key credit issue for new-age brokers despite strong current profitability (Upstox in its 4th profitable year at industry-best margins). Q4 exit run-rate ~+40% Q4-vs-Q4 suggests recovery, but margins are guided to normalise lower as models diversify into capital-consuming NBFC/MTF businesses. Track F&O share of revenue and MTF book funding structure
● WATCH
Zaggle guided 25-30% standalone and ~40% consolidated FY27 revenue growth with consolidated cash-flow break-even targeted this year, but management itself flagged that Propel voucher redemptions are booked gross - investors must value net monetisation, not reported revenue
The gross-vs-net accounting caveat on Propel (57% of revenue) means headline growth overstates economic value; software (2% of revenue) and program fees (41%) are the higher-quality streams expected to drive ~50% of incremental growth. For credit, watch the operating-cash-flow break-even milestone and working-capital intensity around the 19-bank partnership model
Other Takeaways
● POSITIVE
Merchant-lending asset quality across fintech platforms has structurally improved - Paytm's expected credit loss is declining, Mobikwik slippages have fallen for two quarters, and daily-repayment merchant cash advance products show materially better performance than monthly-EMI books
Reduces tail risk for banks/NBFCs funding fintech-originated merchant books and for co-lending partners. Daily deduction from settlement flows aligns repayment with cash generation (15-20% stated rate translating to ~30% IRR for Paytm). Track platform-level ECL/slippage disclosures and FLDG invocation rates quarterly
● POSITIVE
The FLDG regime has stabilised as a risk-sharing mechanism: guarantees capped at 5%, Mobikwik's outstanding FLDG losses down ~35% YoY with clawbacks contributing, and 70-75% of its lending still FLDG-based while it migrates toward co-lending under its new NBFC licence
For partner banks/NBFCs the capped-FLDG plus co-lending shift means cleaner, regulated risk transfer versus the pre-2023 uncapped model. Watch the pace of migration from FLDG to co-lending and whether Mobikwik keeps the model asset-light rather than balance-sheet-heavy; metric: share of lending under FLDG vs co-lending
● POSITIVE
Creditwise Capital is an emerging 2W NBFC credit story: CRAR 27%, leverage 3x, CRISIL BBB with BBB+ upgrade expected, CoF 10.5%, credit cost 2.5-2.6% recognised conservatively at 360 DPD (industry writes off at 720 DPD), and institutional validation via HDFC Bank co-lending and an ICICI Bank term sheet for equity
The 360-DPD write-off policy means reported credit cost understates the gap versus peers reporting at 720 DPD - a genuine underwriting edge (75-80% rejection rate). Risks: geographic concentration (50% Bihar+Maharashtra), FY25 disbursement growth of only 6% after Haryana disruptions, and an ambitious INR 50bn AUM target requiring the nascent LAP vertical (INR 250mn today). Track post-raise net worth (INR 4.5bn planned) and rating upgrade to BBB+
● POSITIVE
Merchant payments monetisation is structurally improving - margins up from ~3bps to ~4bps and heading to ~5bps on rationalised industry pricing - giving payments platforms a self-funding acquisition engine even without UPI MDR, whose introduction remains uncertain
Better payment unit economics reduce cash burn and reliance on lending income across Paytm, PhonePe, Pine Labs and Mobikwik, supporting standalone viability. UPI MDR would be pure upside not baked into plans. Track Paytm's mid-50s contribution margin guidance and 20-25% FY27 revenue growth as the sector bellwether
● POSITIVE
Banking infrastructure and RegTech vendors (M2P, IDfy) are approaching profitability inflection with strong secular demand as banks consolidate technology vendors - M2P targets 25-30% EBITDA margin in 2-3 years with a listing in 3-4; IDfy targets INR 8-10bn revenue at 30-40% EBITDA margin (from INR 2.5bn at ~5%)
Recurring, compliance-driven revenue with 8-10 year build barriers makes these attractive future issuers, but both are pre-scale on margins and M2P has cut headcount 1,800 to 1,300 to reach break-even. Ambitious 3-4x revenue targets embed M&A execution risk (IDfy has an acquisition war chest). ● POSITIVE

The DiGiSPICE-Spice Money merger simplifies the listed structure around the profitable fintech core. Track AEPS market share and credit-business break-even

What Can I Learn
  • Write-off/DPD recognition timing makes credit-cost comparisons across NBFCs misleading: CreditWise's 2.5-2.6% credit cost at 360-DPD write-off is more conservative than an identical number at the industry-standard 720 DPD - always normalise the recognition timeline before benchmarking
  • Headline GNPA improvement can be transaction-driven, not operational: Moneyboxx's GNPA drop from 6.61% to 3.59% was aided by an ARC sale, and its secured book has only ~2 years of vintage - adjust for sales and seasoning before crediting an underwriting turnaround
  • Repayment mechanics are an underwriting variable: daily auto-deduction from merchant settlement flows (Paytm's merchant cash advance) structurally outperforms monthly EMI products because collection is embedded in the cash cycle - product design can substitute for borrower quality
  • Gross-vs-net revenue accounting matters for platform credit analysis: Zaggle's Propel voucher redemptions are booked gross, so leverage and coverage metrics built on reported revenue overstate true monetisation - anchor analysis on net take-rate economics

YES Securities Q1FY27 Earnings Preview: SFBs, NBFCs, HFCs & Credit Rating AgenciesNEW

01 Jul 2026 · Broker Research · Sector Report · SFBs-NBFCs-HFCs-&-CRAs_Q1FY27-Earnings-Preview (1).pdf
📌 Crux of the Report
  • YES Securities (analyst Rajiv Mehta, signed 1 Jul 2026) expects a resilient Q1FY27 for its SFB/NBFC/HFC/CRA coverage, with MFIs and SFBs delivering the strongest results, followed by Affordable HFCs and Vehicle Financiers; the West Asia conflict (energy/commodity price surge, supply-chain disruption) has had no meaningful impact on low/mid-income household debt servicing due to controlled leverage, weak inflation transmission and no significant job/migration losses.
  • Microfinance is the standout: X-bucket collection efficiency held up despite TN/WB state elections and heat waves, 1-90 dpd collections stayed resilient, write-offs are declining, and new loan pricing above portfolio yield (price hikes + risk-based pricing) should lift NIMs — positive surprises seen from Ujjivan SFB, CreditAccess Grameen, Fusion, Aptus and Home First.
  • Expected YoY PAT growth (Exhibit 2/5): CreditAccess +606.7% (PAT Rs4.1bn), Manappuram +293% (Rs5.2bn), Ujjivan SFB +179% (Rs2.9bn), Muthoot +79.3% (Rs35.4bn on NII +54.3%), Shriram Finance +53.2% (Rs33bn, NII +28.2%), MMFS +44.9%, Chola +26.4% (PAT Rs14.4bn), Bajaj Finance +25.9% (PAT Rs60bn, NII Rs150.5bn +19.4%); laggards are Repco (-1.9%) and Spandana (still loss-making at Rs-52mn); Equitas and Fusion swing back from year-ago losses.
  • Sequential AUM growth leaders (Exhibit 1): Spandana 6.5%, Home First 6.1%, Bajaj Finance 5.6%, Manappuram 5.3%, Ujjivan 4.7%, Chola 4.6%; tail-enders SBI Card 0.4%, LIC HF 0.5%, Muthoot 1.0% — gold loan growth moderating on tighter new regulations (interest loaded into LTV calculation, borrower repayment-capacity assessment for loans >Rs2.5 lakh), higher competition and late-quarter gold price softness.
  • Annualized credit cost estimates (Exhibit 3): SBI Card highest at 8.2%, CreditAccess 3.7%, Shriram 2.1%, MMFS 2.1%, Fusion 2.0%, Chola 1.9%, Five Star 1.8%, Bajaj Finance 1.6%; HFCs near-zero (Aavas 0.2%, LIC HF 0.1%, Repco 0.1%) — asset quality expected to show only seasonal movement with no additional flows from war, inflation or weather.
  • Vehicle finance: PV/Tractor retail strongest, MHCV hit from May by fuel price hike and fuel-availability uncertainty; MMFS benefits from PV/Tractor exposure, Chola from non-vehicle diversification, Shriram sees transient Used-CV slowdown but NIM improvement of 50-60bps from the large equity infusion at the start of Q1FY27.
  • Affordable HFCs: disbursement growth resurrecting via distribution addition, pricing/ticket-size refinement and strong LAP growth; balance-transfer-out stable at moderate levels, but spreads to compress from recent PLR cuts and lower new-loan pricing with CoF broadly stable.
  • Credit Rating Agencies (Exhibit 4): bond issuances tepid on higher rates but bank credit to large corporates and NBFC/HFCs accelerated; surveillance-fee growth healthy and fee caps mute mix-shift impact on realizations — expected YoY: CRISIL revenue +14.9%/EBITDA +15.3%/PAT +13.1%, CARE +14.5%/+22.6%/+19.4%, ICRA revenue +30.5%/EBITDA +26.4% but PAT -6.4% with continued Knowledge Services challenges.
⚠ Risk Analysis 4
● WATCH
Gold loan NBFCs face a growth inflection: sequential portfolio growth moderates sharply for Muthoot (AUM +1.0% QoQ) as new RBI rules — interest loaded into LTV computation and mandatory repayment-capacity assessment for loans >Rs2.5 lakh — create on-ground friction, even as current-quarter profitability stays strong (Muthoot PAT +79.3%, Manappuram +293%).
Peak profitability on trailing AUM growth masks a forward growth/yield/opex squeeze from regulation, competition and gold price softness. Track sequential gold AUM growth and portfolio yield trajectory over the next 2-3 quarters; rising BT activity would signal pricing power erosion.
● NEGATIVE
SBI Card remains the credit-quality outlier with an 8.2% annualized credit cost estimate — roughly 2.2x the next highest (CreditAccess 3.7%) — combined with the weakest sequential receivables growth (0.4%) and muted PAT growth of 7.9%.
Unsecured revolver stress persists even as secured/MFI books heal, showing this cycle is asset-class-specific rather than systemic. Track SBI Card's credit cost and net flow into Stage 2/3; sustained 8%+ credit cost compresses risk-adjusted margins and internal capital generation.
● WATCH
Affordable HFCs face margin compression: spreads come under pressure from PLR cuts taken in recent months and lower new-loan pricing while cost of funds stays flat — growth (Home First +6.1% QoQ AUM, PAT +22.3%) is being partly bought at thinner spreads and via LAP.
In a falling-rate cycle, asset-side repricing leads liability-side relief for NBFC-HFCs, squeezing spreads near-term; rising LAP mix subtly raises portfolio risk. Track incremental spreads and LAP share of disbursements; stable BT-out rates confirm competition is not intensifying indirectly.
● NEGATIVE
Tail-risk names remain visible within the recovery: Spandana is still loss-making (Rs-52mn PAT, revenue barely +1.4% YoY despite 6.5% QoQ AUM growth) and Repco's PAT declines 1.9% YoY — the sector recovery is broad but not universal.
Growth restarts before profitability at stressed franchises; Spandana growing AUM 6.5% QoQ while unprofitable warrants scrutiny of underwriting discipline. Track Spandana's credit cost (1.3% annualized estimate) and pre-provision operating profit trajectory (down 117.3% YoY) for franchise viability.
Other Takeaways
● POSITIVE
The microfinance credit cycle has decisively turned: with X-bucket CE resilient through elections and heat waves, declining write-offs and shrinking delinquency buckets, credit costs fall further sequentially — driving outsized PAT normalization (CreditAccess +606.7%, Ujjivan +179%, Fusion back to Rs619mn profit).
MFI lenders' loss-absorption buffers rebuild as NIMs expand (new loan pricing above portfolio yield) while credit cost normalizes; residual risk is event-driven (state-level ordinances, weather). Track X-bucket collection efficiency and CreditAccess's annualized credit cost (still elevated at 3.7%) for confirmation the cycle trough is behind.
● NEUTRAL
The West Asia conflict's energy/commodity shock has surprisingly NOT impaired debt servicing of low and mid-income households — leverage is controlled, inflation transmission weak, and no significant migration/job losses — so no additional asset-quality flows beyond seasonality across the coverage.
A key macro-overhang for retail lenders is currently benign, but it is an assumption resting on limited inflation pass-through; a sustained fuel/commodity price rise would first show in MHCV operator cash flows (already hit from May) and MFI household budgets. Track fuel prices and 1+ dpd formation in vehicle finance and MFI books.
● POSITIVE

● NEUTRAL
Vehicle financier fortunes diverge by asset mix: MHCV retail sales fell from May on fuel price hikes and fuel-availability uncertainty, while PV, Tractor, 2W and LCV stayed healthy-to-strong — favouring MMFS (PAT +44.9%) and Chola (PAT +26.4%, AUM +4.6% QoQ) over CV-heavy books.
Fleet-operator cash flow is the transmission channel from fuel prices to NBFC asset quality; a prolonged MHCV downcycle would lift Stage 2 in CV books. Track monthly MHCV retail registrations and CV-segment collection efficiency at Shriram and Chola.
● POSITIVE
For CRAs, the revenue engine has rotated from bond issuance (tepid on higher rates) to bank loan ratings — bank credit to large corporates and NBFC/HFCs accelerated — with fee caps in larger clients muting any adverse mix impact; domestic ratings margin expansion continues (CARE EBITDA +22.6% vs revenue +14.5%).
Surveillance fees (bulk of rating revenue) provide annuity-like stability, making CRA earnings resilient to capital-market cycles. Track corporate bond issuance volumes as rates ease — a bond revival would be an incremental kicker; watch ICRA's PAT decline (-6.4% YoY) driven by Knowledge Services, a diversification cautionary tale.
● POSITIVE
Full-year guidance risk is skewed positive for select names: strong Q1 execution by Chola and Bajaj Finance (PAT Rs60bn, +25.9%, AUM +5.6% QoQ) would augment confidence in their FY27 growth outlook/guidance, while positive earnings surprises are flagged for Ujjivan SFB, CreditAccess, Fusion, Aptus and Home First against extant valuations.
Guidance-affirming quarters lower funding costs and widen investor access for these issuers. Track disbursement growth momentum (expected strong for a second successive quarter for MFIs/SFBs) and any guidance revisions on Q1 calls.

What Can I Learn
  • Retail credit cycles in India are increasingly asset-class-specific, not systemic: in the same quarter, MFI credit costs normalize sharply (CreditAccess PAT +607%) while credit-card credit cost sits at 8.2% — portfolio surveillance must be segmented by product, not by 'retail NBFC' as a bloc.
  • Regulation can flip a sector's credit narrative faster than the macro: gold loan NBFCs post peak profits (Muthoot PAT +79%) exactly when LTV-computation and repayment-assessment rules choke forward growth to 1% QoQ — trailing profitability is a lagging indicator when the rulebook changes.
  • In a rate-cut cycle, floating-rate lenders (AHFCs taking PLR cuts) see spread compression before CoF relief, while fixed-rate/short-duration lenders (MFIs repricing new loans above portfolio yield) see NIM expansion — the direction of margin impact depends on repricing asymmetry, not the rate move itself.
  • A geopolitical commodity shock transmits to retail asset quality only through specific channels — fuel-dependent fleet operators (MHCV) cracked first while low-income household books stayed clean due to weak inflation pass-through; map the transmission channel before assuming broad-based stress.

● NEUTRAL
The Nov 2025 overhaul is architectural, not a tightening — all binding prudential numbers (15% CRAR, Rs 1,000 cr ML threshold, 90-day NPA, concentration caps) are preserved, so no rating actions flow from the re-issuance itself, but committee citations must now reference the specific themed MD, not the repealed 2023 SBR MD.
Continuity of obligations is saved by transition clauses; the credit-relevant change is eight new/consolidated themed regimes (Climate, RBC, Concentration, Credit Risk, Resolution, ALM, Transfer, Investment Portfolio). Track issuer compliance gap analyses against the new instruments in FY27 disclosures.
● POSITIVE
The Dividends MD is a built-in weakness disclosure: full 50% payout (60% CIC/SPD) requires CRAR compliance and NNPA <6% in each of the last 3 years; an issuer capped at 10% payout is being force-retained by the regulator — read payout behaviour as a solvency signal.
The eligibility test converts capital/asset-quality history into an observable behaviour. A sudden payout jump to 50% can signal cyclical peak earnings or expected growth deceleration; a 10%-capped issuer failed the 3-year test. Track payout ratio vs the eligibility matrix annually alongside Section 45-IC statutory reserve transfers (20% of net profit).

What Can I Learn
  • Regulatory ratios are floors, not comfort: an issuer 'compliant but tight' on multiple metrics (24% single-borrower vs 25% cap, CRAR at 15.3%) is one bad quarter from cascading breaches — rate the aggregate margin of safety, not each ratio's pass/fail.
  • Triangulate three views of the same issuer: what management discloses, what the auditor certifies to RBI (NBS-2), and what the regulator sees (DNBS-02, CRILC SMA data). Inconsistencies between them are themselves the rating signal, often ahead of the financials.
  • Capital quality beats capital quantity: strip PDIs, revaluation reserves, DTAs and group double-counting to get equity-only Tier-1 and true NOF before comparing peers — the Rs 14 cr NOF haircut in a Rs 40 cr Owned Fund worked example shows how a model using headline net worth overstates buffers by a third.
  • Off-book is not off-risk: co-lending FLDGs, retained securitisation tranches, PCE, undrawn committed lines and letters of comfort all absorb capital or crystallise under stress; rate origination volume and contingent exposures, not just the balance-sheet loan book.

ARC Analyst Handbook: RBI Asset Reconstruction Companies Directions, 2025NEW

28 Nov 2025 · Regulatory · NBFC & FI · ARC-Analyst-Handbook-Nov2025.html
📌 Crux of the Report
  • 20-chapter reference handbook distilling the RBI (Asset Reconstruction Companies) Directions, 2025 (DoR.FIN.REC.No.293/26.03.001/2025-26, dated 28 Nov 2025, updated to 10 Mar 2026); ARCs are regulated under SARFAESI (Sections 3, 9, 10, 12, 12A), not the RBI Act, and a single CoR covers both securitisation and asset reconstruction.
  • Capital gateway: minimum NOF of ₹300 crore (ongoing; legacy ARCs had until 31 Mar 2026), with a March 2026 amendment letting quarterly profits count toward Owned Fund via EPt = NPt − 0.25×D×t; the IBC Resolution Applicant role needs ₹1,000 crore NOF plus a 5-year control-exit sunset; deposit-taking is absolutely prohibited and bilateral acquisitions from sponsors/lenders/group entities are banned (open auctions permitted).
  • Acquisition and realisation mechanics: Board-approved acquisition policy within 90 days of CoR, at least 2 weeks pre-bid diligence, a 60%-by-value secured-creditor consent threshold for enforcement, a 6-month Planning Period (assets treated as standard), and a 5-year base / 8-year maximum realisation ceiling that carries over unreset even on ARC-to-ARC secondary sales.
  • Reconstruction toolkit: management change/takeover needs the ARC's claim ≥25% of borrower total assets plus 60% SR-holder consent, IAC review, 60-day notice and a reasoned Board order in 30 days; settlements above ₹1 crore require IAC-plus-independent-director committee governance (fraud/wilful-defaulter cases regardless of size, with a Para 62 NPV-≥-realisable-security-value floor); debt-to-equity conversion is capped at 26% of post-conversion equity unless a four-condition exemption (half-independent Board, majority-ID committee, monthly MTM, NOF compliance) is met.
  • Security Receipts: privately placed, QB-only, generally below-investment-grade hybrids issued via ARC-trusteed trusts; the ARC must retain the higher of 15% of transferors' SR investment or 2.5% of total SRs per class until full redemption; recovery ratings (a recovery-percentage band, not a default rating) are due within 6 months of acquisition, reviewed each 30 June/31 December with 6-cycle (3-year) minimum CRA retention; NAV = face value × the ARC's chosen point within the CRA band (judgement risk).
  • Prudential rules: single CRAR of NOF/RWA ≥15% with a flat 100% risk weight on virtually all core assets (0% only on cash/G-secs/other-ARC shares, 50% on contingents); the ARC's own NPA clock is 180 days (vs 90 for NBFCs) with sub-standard ≤12 months, doubtful thereafter, and automatic Loss at 36 months NPA or on breach of the 5/8-year realisation ceiling; provisioning is security-coverage-driven — 10% sub-standard, 100% on the uncovered doubtful portion plus 50% on the covered portion, 100%/write-off for Loss, and no standard-asset provision.
  • Earnings quality is deliberately suppressed: SR yield/upside is recognised only after full redemption of the entire SR principal (no accrual), and management fee — computed on NAV at the lower end of the CRA range, capped at acquisition value, payable only from recoveries — must be reversed if unrealised within 180 days or immediately if SR NAV falls below 50% of face value, with unrealised fee deducted from NOF for Ind-AS ARCs; ECL runs against an IRACP prudential floor via a non-distributable Impairment Reserve.
  • Governance and disclosure: prior RBI approval for every director/MD-CEO (90-day PRAVAAH lead time), mandatory independent Board Chair for all ARCs, MD/CEO caps of 70 years age / 5-year terms / 15-year continuous tenure with 3-year cooling-off, sponsor fit-and-proper with FATF-jurisdiction investors capped below 20% voting power; the Para 150 schedule mandates 18 balance-sheet disclosures (including auto-flags for >20% disposal discounts and >20% SR value declines), plus monthly public SARFAESI-possession disclosure and an FPC with 8am–7pm recovery calling hours and ₹5,000/day document-release delay compensation.
⚠ Risk Analysis 6
● WATCH
An ARC below ₹300 crore NOF faces prohibition on incremental business — existential for a pure-recovery model — and the quarterly-profit inclusion route (EPt = NPt − 0.25×D×t, March 2026 amendment) makes Owned Fund composition more volatile and audit-dependent.
NOF is the single capital gateway (no SBR-style layers) and cross-holdings in group entities or other ARCs beyond 10% of Owned Fund are netted out, so a strong headline Owned Fund can collapse into a weak NOF. Track NOF trend versus ₹300 crore and the share of NOF resting on quarterly profits rather than paid-up capital.
● NEGATIVE
The 5-year base / 8-year maximum realisation ceiling is a forcing function: any asset (including via ARC-to-ARC sale, which inherits the original clock) unrealised past the ceiling is automatically classified Loss and fully provided/written off regardless of collateral quality.
A book with heavy vintages approaching year 5-8 faces a wall of compulsory write-offs even on genuinely recoverable assets; routine Board extensions to 8 years also delay QBs' Section 7(3) enforcement rights. Track the vintage distribution of acquired assets against the 5/8-year clock and the share of Loss classifications that are timeline-driven versus collateral-driven.
● WATCH
Capital adequacy is mechanically fragile: a single 15% CRAR test on NOF with a flat 100% risk weight on essentially the whole core asset base means CRAR compresses near-1:1 with book growth and offers almost no risk-weight-mix management lever.
Growth is capital-intensive and CRAR tells you about capital, not asset quality (unlike NBFCs where RW mix encodes quality). Track CRAR headroom above 15% against planned acquisition volume, plus NOF-eroding items: provisioning hits, unrealised-fee deductions, and fair-value losses (gains are excluded, losses included — asymmetric).
● WATCH
SR recovery ratings are not credit ratings and NAV embeds management judgement: NAV = face value × a percentage the ARC itself selects within a ±10pp-wide CRA recovery band, reviewed only half-yearly (30 June/31 December).
For any lender holding SRs, published NAV is a judgement-laden, lagging number — two ARCs with identical CRA bands can report different NAVs. Track recovery-rating migration across half-yearly reviews and whether the ARC consistently picks the top of its band without realised-collection support; CRA changes inside the mandatory 6-cycle (3-year) retention window are a rating-shopping red flag.
● WATCH
Doubtful-asset provisioning swings entirely on the 'estimated realisable value of security' — 100% on the uncovered portion plus 50% on the covered portion — making collateral valuation quality a live provisioning input every period, not just an acquisition-stage control.
An ARC carrying stale or optimistic realisable-value assumptions can materially understate provisions (e.g., ₹100 cr doubtful at 60% coverage needs ₹70 cr provision; at 30% coverage, ₹85 cr). Track provision coverage ratio trend, disclosed covered/uncovered splits, and revaluation recency on large doubtful exposures.
● WATCH
Related-party and captive-vehicle risk is the framework's central anti-abuse theme: bilateral acquisitions from sponsor banks, ARC lenders/fund subscribers and group entities are banned (auctions only), management fee may be paid only out of actual recoveries, and enforcement-acquired land/buildings must be disposed of within 5 years.
Historic ARC failure modes are sponsor-captive balance-sheet laundering and becoming an accidental real-estate/PE vehicle. Track Para 150 Item 1 seller concentration (dominance of a single sponsor bank even via 'auctions' warrants auction-design scrutiny), year-wise land/building holdings against the 5-year window, and debt-conversion equity stakes as a percentage of NOF.
Other Takeaways
● NEUTRAL
ARC reported earnings structurally lag economic recovery: SR yield/upside is recognised only after full redemption of the entire SR principal, and management fee reverses if unrealised in 180 days or instantly when SR NAV drops below 50% of face value — with unrealised fee deducted from NOF for Ind-AS ARCs.
A young ARC can show thin profits while building real recovery value, and conversely a fee-heavy P&L can be hollow if collections lag. Track the Para 150(18) unrealised management-fee ageing (especially >1-year and >3-year buckets) and scheme-level collection efficiency against realisation plans, not just reported P&L.
● NEUTRAL
The 180-day regulatory NPA clock (twice the NBFC 90-day standard) coexists with Ind-AS 109's 90-day default presumption, so Ind-AS ARCs generally recognise Stage 3 impairment earlier than regulatory NPA — any later default definition needs ACB approval and disclosure, and SICR recognition cannot be deferred beyond 60 days past due.
The accounting-versus-regulatory gap is a disclosure-rich diagnostic: the Annex VI ECL-vs-IRACP comparison and the Impairment Reserve (non-distributable, non-capital) reveal whether ECL models are running lighter than the prudential floor. Track the size and trend of Impairment Reserve appropriations and disclosed >90-day-not-impaired accounts.
● POSITIVE
Governance requirements exceed the NBFC middle-layer standard — universal independent Board Chair, prior RBI approval for every director appointment, 70-year age cap, 5-year terms with a 15-year continuous-tenure ceiling and 3-year cooling-off, and NRC scrutiny extending to sponsors — because ARC economics rest on hard-to-verify judgement calls (NAV selection, settlement NPV, takeover grounds).
Governance quality is the most reliable differentiator between ARCs with similar headline metrics. Track independent-chair genuineness, MD/CEO tenure against the 15-year ceiling with succession planning, IAC member independence, and whether fraud/wilful-defaulter settlements get full Tier-1 committee governance irrespective of size.
● NEUTRAL
The Para 150 schedule of 18 mandatory balance-sheet disclosures is the richest single diagnostic on any ARC, including automatic threshold-triggered flags: assets disposed at >20% discount to prior-year valuation and SRs whose value has declined >20% below acquisition value.
Read items together: acquisition (Item 5) vs realisation (Item 6) vs backlog (Item 7) trended over 3+ years reveals whether the pipeline is outrunning recovery capacity; Items 13-14 surface material valuation misses without digging; monthly public SARFAESI-possession disclosure on the ARC's website is a free primary-source cross-check on specific exposures.

What Can I Learn
  • For ARCs, always check the realisation-timeline clock before collateral analysis: the 5/8-year ceiling silently drives mandatory Loss classification — the most severe consequence in the framework — and secondary ARC-to-ARC sales inherit, not reset, the original 8-year clock.
  • Treat SR NAV and recovery ratings as judgement-laden estimates, not observed prices: NAV is the ARC's chosen point inside a ±10pp CRA band updated only half-yearly, so build an independent recovery model for any material SR exposure and track band-selection behaviour across schemes.
  • ARC earnings analysis inverts NBFC analysis: with SR yield locked until full redemption and management fee on an aggressive 180-day/NAV-below-50% reversal regime, judge the franchise on scheme-level collection efficiency against realisation plans, not reported P&L.
  • An ARC's CRAR carries no asset-quality information — the flat 100% risk weight makes RWA nearly proportional to gross book size — so asset quality must be read separately through sub-standard-to-doubtful migration rates and security-coverage-driven provisioning adequacy.
